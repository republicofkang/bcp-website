Passive Investing Quickstart Checklist

7 steps to making your first (or next) passive investment.

1.Get Clear On Your Investment Goals

Do you like real estate as an investment vehicle? When you invest in real estate, every dollar can work for you in more than one way. Your money can:

  • Create cashflow
  • Create equity growth
  • Benefit from market appreciation
  • Benefit from leveraged returns
  • Benefit from pass through tax benefits

Do you want to be in a passive role versus an active role?

  • Active investingmeans you are finding, qualifying and closing on the asset and overseeing the business plan. This path is great if you have the time, knowledge, hustle and access to capital.
  • Passive investingcan be a powerful strategy to build passive cash flow and long-term wealth AND have your time, freedom and attention to do other things. You can still take advantage of all 5 wealth generators of real estate (see above.)

If you want to be passive, what do you want from your passive investing?

  • Do you want cash flow, appreciation, or a balanced blend of both?There are investments that offer cash flow NOW, or ~2X equity growth over 5-7 years, with risk profiles LOWER than the stock market.

Why do you want to invest passively?

  • Do you want more time with your family, make your JOB optional, travel more, etc.?

Do you have capital to invest?Most investments start with a minimum $50K investment.
